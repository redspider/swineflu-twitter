What is Swine Influenza?

Swine Influenza is the common name now given to a new type of Flu virus, derived in part from human influenzavirus type A, subtype H1N1, and in part from strains of influenza virus normally found only in pigs.

The new virus appears to transmit from human to human, and indications so far suggest it is capable of becoming a pandemic.

What's the risk?

At the moment the risk factor seems low. While quarantine procedures seem unlikely to prevent the eventual spread of the virus, they are having some success. More importantly, relatively few individuals are known to have died from the disease, and indeed it seems rare that sufferers even require hospitalisation.

The Tamiflu antiviral appears effective and New Zealand is reasonably well supplied. Following the preparation checklist below is useful for all potential disaster scenarios.

Prevention

Preventing infection
Avoid close contact
Avoid close contact with people who are sick. When you are sick, keep your distance from others to help protect them.
Stay home when sick
If at all possible, stay home from work, school and errands when you are sick to help prevent others from catching your illness.
Cover your mouth and nose
When coughing or sneezing, cover your mouth and nose with a tissue. Dispose of tissues promptly and carefully.
Clean your hands
Wash your hands often. Alcohol-based hand sanitisers are effective.
Avoid touching your eyes, nose or mouth
Germs are often spread when a person touches something that is contaiminated, then touches his or her eyes, nose or mouth.
Practice other good health habits
Get plenty of sleep, be physically active, manage stress, drink plenty of fluids and eat nutrious food.

Prevention questions

Can eating pork result in infection?
No. Eating properly handled and cooked pork and pork products is safe. Cooking pork to an internal temperature of 160°F (72°C) kills the swine flu virus as it does other bacteria and viruses.
Will Tamiflu or Relenza help?
Yes, Tamiflu and Relenza, both antivirals, appear to be effective against this strain at this time.
Will antibiotics help?
No, antibiotics are only effective against bacteria, not viruses. Your Doctor may prescribe antibiotics to deal with secondary infections during Treatment.
Will natural remedies help?
There are no known natural remedies capable of combating a virus directly. Secondary effects, such as improving immune response, are unknown.
I was vaccinated against the normal flu, will this help?
Definitive information is not yet available, but general expert consensus seems to be that the vaccinations given for the current seasonal flu will not prevent infection by Swine Influenza.
Will wearing a mask help?
Masks can help reduce the risk infection, both directly and by preventing the wearer from touching the nose or mouth. They must be used correctly however, and are only a limited solution. Masks should be available from your pharmacist.

Symptoms + Treatment

How do I know if I've got it?

Swine Influenza can cause a wide range of symptoms, including fever, cough, sore throat, body aches, headache, chills and fatigue. Some people have reported diarrhea and vomiting associated with swine flu. As with regular flu, Swine Influenza can vary form mild to severe: pneumonia, respiratory failure and even death is possible.

It takes between 1 and 3 days for the infection to become apparent.

When to seek medical attention

When presenting with any flu symptoms, you should telephone your normal medical professional or (NZ) Healthline on 0800 611 116 (Do not just turn up at the waiting room, you may infect others).

In the event of any of the following, you should seek emergency medical care immediately:

  • Difficulty breathing or chest pain
  • Purple or blue discolouration of the lips
  • Vomiting and unable to keep liquids down
  • Signs of dehydration such as dizziness when standing, absence of urination, or in infants, a lack of tears when they cry
  • Less responsive than normal or becomes confused
In children, the following additional warning signs apply:
  • Fast breathing or trouble breathing
  • Bluish skin color
  • Not drinking enough fluids
  • Not waking up or not interacting
  • Being so irritable that the child does not want to be held
  • Flu-like symptoms improve but then return with a fever and worse cough
  • Fever with a rash
If in doubt, seek care. Better safe than sorry.

Important home treatment notes

The CDC has a comprehensive home care guide
Please see Interim Guidance for Swine Influenza: Taking Care of a Sick Person in Your Home
Do NOT use medicines containing asprin for children or teenagers
Use of asprin to treat symptoms of viral illnesses increases the risk of developing Reye's Syndrome. Ibuprofen and similar are ok.
Stay at home for 7 days after symptoms cease
In order to avoid exposing others to the virus, you should remain at home for 7 days after your symptoms have ceased. For children, this should be extended to 10 days.

Preparation

Preparation can significantly improve your comfort and reduce the chances of infection in the event of a pandemic by removing your need to leave home for supplies.

Items short list

  • Torch, preferably several, at least one close to bed
  • Radio
  • First aid kit and essential medicines
  • Blankets or sleeping bags
  • Emergency contacts list
  • Spare batteries
  • Copies of important documents such as birth certificates, passports, insurance documents etc..
  • A primus or gas barbeque to cook on
  • Can opener x 2
  • Supplies of ibuprofen for all the family (available in bottles of 100)
  • Tissues and sealable plastic bags to dispose of tissues and face masks
  • Face masks and rubber gloves
  • Candles (slow burning) and Matches. Matches matches matches.
  • Books and other non-electronic entertainment
  • Plenty of toilet paper, paper towels
  • Some cash in small bills in a safe place
  • A copy of medical history, allergies for each individual
  • Non-scented bleach, alcohol wipes, soap
  • Child supplies: Food, formula, diapers, medicines
  • Paper plates, cups, disposable silverware
  • Multivitamins
  • Water purifiers
  • Water filter
  • Water containers (hard, clear plastic)
  • Feminine hygiene products
  • Thermal underwear
  • Garbage bags (lots)
  • Fire extinguisher
  • Paper, pads, pencils
  • Duct tape
  • Backup glasses for visually impaired
  • Cigarettes (if smoker)
  • Keep vehicle petrol tank full
