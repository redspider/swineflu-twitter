Notes

The dashboard provides a realtime view into the swine-flu related chatter of a large global population. Much of the chatter is humourous, pointless or even blatently incorrect, and due to the nature of twitter regularly duplicated. The conversation does have value however:

Up to date
The conversation is almost certain to reference to breaking news of general interest, and the more interesting, the more duplicates and linking will occur, making it easier to spot within the noise.
Broad selection
While the individuals are self-selected and biased towards technically capable individuals, the barrier to "Tweeting" is low and as such the general tone of the conversation is a useful barometer for the current state of public opinion.
Link aggregation
A wide variety of useful links are being put up on the topic each day. Finding and evaluating these is an impossibly large task, however keeping half an eye on links being posted by twitter users helps ensure that the most important of these reach your attention in a timely fashion.

Evaluation

In order to make a basic evaluation of the current attitude, it is useful to simply sit and read for a while. In general, it seems possible to observe a regular (and somewhat predictable) pattern - those unaffected are for the most part calm or even derisive of the attention the issue is recieving. As it comes closer to affecting them, turning up nearby to them, friends or family, the attitude becomes more serious and concerned.

More specific concerns are easily identified. Many feel the issue is distracting from other issues important to them, many are confused that the issue is attracting so much attention when other issues of proven fatality are ignored (common comparisons with regular flu, HIV, car crashes). While in the early stages of the conversation hand washing receiving significant attention, in general the symbol of preventative measures has now become the face mask.

Future

These are almost notes to myself, but if anyone else ends up building a similar tool, it's a wishlist of things I'd like to have.

Dashboard filtering
It would be useful to be able to filter the dashboard by various things, notably region. It is possible to modify the twitter search to restrict it to messages from, say, New Zealand, which would help in evaluating the mood in a given locale.
Pause, play, rewind
Some basic playback controls on the dashboard allowing the user to pause in order to copy&paste a current message, or rewind to review something they just missed, would be very useful.
Evaluation buttons
A modified form of this site could utilise the power of a large number of viewers to tag messages with, say, "Useful", "Informative", "Funny", "Important". This information could then be used to generate a summary dashboard with the more relevant information to reduce the workload on skilled analysts.
Additional sources
Additional data sources, notably Flickr, Google News and even YouTube could be integrated.
Smarter dashboard
A few additional minor features, including decoding links into their full form to make them easier to evaluate, being able to select from a number of different columns rather than just 3 fixed ones and additional user information such as location
Auto-responders
While not appropriate in a public tool, a private instance of the same system could prompt the operator for canned replies to certain messages on twitter. This could allow health advice groups to respond to individuals posting concerns, queries and mis-information in a fast, efficient fashion. Probably only useful within limited geographic areas.
